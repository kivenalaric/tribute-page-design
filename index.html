Dr Norman Borluag

The man who saved a billion lives

Dr Norman Borlaug, hird from the left, trains biologist in mexico on how to increase wheat yeilds -part of his life-long war on hunger.

Here's a line of Dr. Borlaug's life:


  • 1914 - Born in Cresco, lowa
  • 1933 - Leaves his Family,s farm to attend the University of Minnesota, theanks to a depression era program known as the "National Youth Administration".
  • 1935 - Has to stop school and save up more money. Works in the Civilian Conservation Corps, helping starving Americans. "I saw how food changed them", he said. "All of this left scars on me."
  • 1937 - Finishes University and takes a job in the US Forestry Service.
  • 1938 - Marries wife of 69 years Margret Gibson. Gets laid of due to budget cuts. Inspired by Elvin Charles Stakman, he returns to school study under Stakman, who teaches him about breeding pest-resistent plants.
  • 1941 - Tries to enroll in the military after the Pearl Hbor attack, but is rejected. Instead, the military asked his lab to work on waterproof dlue, DDT to control malaria, disinfectants, and other applied science.
  • 1942 - Recieves a Ph.D. in Genetics and Plant Pathology
  • 1944 - Born in Cresco, lowa
  • 1945 - Discovers a way to grown wheat twice each season, doubling wheat yeilds
  • 1953 - Born in Cresco, lowa
  • 1962 - Visits Delhi and brings his high-yielding strains of wheat to the indian subcontinent in time to help mitigate mass starvation due to a rapidly expanding population.
  • 1970 - recieves the Nobel Peace Prize.
  • 1983 - helps seven African countries dramatically increase their maize and sorghum yeilds.
  • 1984 - becomes a distinguished professor at Texas A&M University.
  • 2005 - states "we will have to double the world food supply by 2050. " argues that genetically modified crops are the only way we can meet the demand, as we run out of arable land. Says that GM crops are inherently dangerouse because "we've been genetically modifying plants and animals for a long time. Long before we called it science, people were selevting the best breeds."
  • 2009 - dies at the age 95.
